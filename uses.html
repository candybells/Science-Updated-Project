Uses of Tin

Active ingredient in toothpastes: Tin flouride is known for its anti-cavity, anti-plaque, and anti-gingivitis properties, as well as its ability to reduce tooth sensitivity.

Tin Plating in steel cans and food containers: Tin has excellent solderability (ability to connect metals), making it ideal for electrical connections and soldering applications.

Tin is used in ceramic glazes: Tin can react with titanium and rutile to transform the color and character of a glaze. Although tin oxide is a bit more expensive, very little is required to produce stunning effects in many colored glazes.

Tin piping maintains the purity in water and beverages: Tin is a nonreactive metal. This ensures that we would not get poisoning in our drinks.
