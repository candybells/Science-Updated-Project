Tin: The 50th Element

Did you know the Oscar statuettes are really made of tin alloys?

History

The discovery of Tin is unknown, but archaeology evidence has shown that people have been using it for over 5500 years, especially in the Bronze Age in 3000 BC, when people used tin in bronze.

  • Tin’s chemical symbol, Sn, comes from its Latin name, ‘stannum.’
  • The Romans used tin plating on iron and steel to protect it from rust.
  • Napoleon Bonaparte offered a reward in 1795 to anyone who could come up with a way to preserve food for military use. In 1810, French chef Nicolas Appert won the prize by inventing what we now call the tin can.

Fun Facts

If you try bending tin at room temperature, it will produce a screeching sound known as a "tin cry".
Tin has the most stable isotopes out of all the elements because of its atomic number, 50, also known as the "magic number" in nuclear physics.
