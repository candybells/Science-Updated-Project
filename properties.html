Properties of Tin

  • Element Family: Group 14, known as the Carbon Family. Carbon family elements contain atoms that have 4 valence electrons.
  • Atomic Mass: 118.710
  • Density: 7.287g/cm3
  • Temperature Change Points:
    • Boiling Point: 2,602 ºC
    • Melting Point: 231.93 ºC
    • Because tin has such a high melting point, we would normally observe it as a solid at Room Temperature.
  • Reactiveness: Tin does not react quickly with water or air, but it reacts quickly to both strong acids and strong bases as it is quite weak on its own.
  • Exposure: When exposed, an invisible protective film of stannic oxide is formed on tin by the reaction with oxygen in the air

Tin exists in two different forms: the familiar form, white tin, and gray tin, which is powdery and of little use.

Compounds
